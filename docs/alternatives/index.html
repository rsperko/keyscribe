How KeyScribe fits among dictation apps.

KeyScribe is strongest when you want local speech recognition, open-source inspectability, mode routing, correction workflows, and optional BYOK rewrite. This page is a high-level comparison, not a claim that every competitor lacks every feature.

Wispr Flow and AquaStrong on polish, speed, and cross-platform reach. KeyScribe gives up breadth to keep speech recognition local and file-backed.
SuperwhisperA close power-user comparison. KeyScribe leans harder into open-source inspectability, explicit privacy boundaries, and file-backed configuration.
VoiceInk and local toolsOften share the privacy posture. KeyScribe focuses on mode routing, spoken edits, corrections, and a staged local-plus-optional-rewrite pipeline.
Apple DictationFree and built in. KeyScribe adds custom modes, local history, reusable corrections, model choice, and optional rewrite workflows.
MacWhisperBetter suited to file transcription, batches, exports, and diarization workflows. KeyScribe is live dictation first.

Choose KeyScribe if...

  • You want speech recognition to run on your Mac.
  • You do not want telemetry, accounts, or subscription-gated local dictation.
  • You want dictation to route differently for emails, notes, prompts, shell text, and selected-text edits.
  • You want configuration and history stored as local files.
  • You are comfortable with pre-1.0 open-source software.
